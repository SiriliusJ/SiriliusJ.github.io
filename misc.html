PC Builds

Outside research, a small but important part of my skill set is building and maintaining high-performance PCs and lab workstations. Over the years I have built more than 30 desktops and repaired over 50 laptops and lab machines, taking care of OS installation, environment setup, remote access configuration, troubleshooting, and long-term maintenance. For example, I helped a WUSTL EECE lab revive aging workstations by working around obsolete logins and recovering experiment data and legacy engineering software. It started as a hobby but has become a practical infrastructure skill that I hope will directly support the lab I join for my PhD.
